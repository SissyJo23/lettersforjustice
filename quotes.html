Joseph’s Words

His voice, his truth. These are Joseph Lagerman’s own reflections on his plea, his sentence, and his fight for justice.

🗣️ On the Plea

“The ‘plea’ I took was really just them getting me to cop out and avoid trial—on charges they knew probably wouldn’t stick. They amended my first-degree intentional attempted homicide charge—non-stick but daunting—into two separate first-degree recklessly endangering safeties. One was already tacked on with the original charge, along with a deadly weapon modifier. They didn’t put any numbers on it, but the exposure—the sales pitch—was reduced by half if I accepted the plea. I think they covered all their bases to make sure I took the plea ‘knowingly, voluntarily, and intelligently.’ They went through the whole checklist: no meds in the last day, yes this, yes that... But I was definitely a little ill-informed, and maybe a little misled by my public defender. They ended up giving me two and a half years under the max—so legally, not excessive. But that doesn’t make it just.”

🧠 On Mental Health

“I wasn’t trying to hurt anyone. I was scared. I was spiraling. They saw a weapon and ignored the panic. They saw a charge and ignored the diagnosis.”

📣 On Justice

“I want to ensure my voice is heard, my story is told, and that justice is pursued.”

Key Facts

  • 2017 Arrest: Altercation at a gas station. No weapon used, no shots fired, misdemeanor charges only.
  • 2018 Incident: Domestic violence call at home. Joseph discharged one round downward; it struck the door jamb. No one was injured.
  • No prior violent record: Aside from the 2017 misdemeanors, Joseph had no criminal history.
  • Sentence: 32.5 years for a single firearm discharge incident.
  • Appeal: Wisconsin Court of Appeals affirmed the conviction via a no-merit report, without addressing concerns about sentencing enhancements or off-the-record hearings.

Joseph's Story

On January 18, 2018, Joseph had an argument with his then-girlfriend. After the argument escalated and some drinking occurred, Joseph saw figures outside his home and panicked. He reached for his firearm and discharged one round downward, which struck the door jamb. No one was injured.

Joseph was arrested shortly after. Despite the limited danger posed and absence of intent to harm, he received a 32.5-year sentence. Officers present at his sentencing asked for maximum incarceration, and the judge emphasized setting an example due to past fallen officers.

Justice for Joseph Lagerman

A new advocacy campaign, #JoeysLaw, has launched to spotlight the case of Joseph Lagerman, who is currently serving a 32.5-year sentence in Wisconsin for charges stemming from an incident in 2018. Supporters, led by Christy, aim to bring public attention to what they argue are serious judicial oversights and sentencing disparities.

"Joseph's story is more than a legal case — it's about truth, fairness, and human dignity," said Christy. "Through #JoeysLaw, we want to ensure his voice is heard, his story is told, and that justice is pursued."

The Facts

  • Zero injuries: No one was harmed.
  • No intent to harm: Ballistics prove a downward trajectory.
  • Mental health crisis: Diagnosed panic disorder at the time of the incident.
  • No criminal history: Joseph had no prior record.
  • Sentencing disparity: Similar cases average 5–7 years.

Why This Matters

Joseph's case is a chilling example of how the justice system can ignore context, mental health, and proportionality. His sentence doesn't just punish — it erases nuance, compassion, and truth.

No Witnesses. No Trial. Just Time.

Joseph Lagerman never saw his day in court. There were no civilian witnesses. No independent testimony. No chance to challenge the story that was told about him. The plea he entered wasn’t justice—it was fear, pressure, and survival.

And this wasn’t one of many. It was his only case. No priors. No criminal record. Yet the court punished him as if he’d lived a life of violence—stacking every past mistake, every personal struggle, every rumor and fear, into one sentence: 32.5 years.

He was sentenced not for the crime he committed, but for the life they assumed. And still—he’s fighting. And we’re fighting with him.

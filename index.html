The Full Story They Tried to Bury — Joseph’s Words

The “plea” I took was really just a way for them to get me to cop out and avoid trial—a trial they probably knew they couldn’t win. They amended my original first-degree intentional attempted homicide charge (daunting, but shaky) into two counts of first-degree reckless endangerment. One count was already tacked on, plus a deadly weapon modifier. The exposure (their sales pitch) was supposedly cut in half if I accepted the plea.

They made sure I “knowingly, voluntarily, and intelligently” took the plea by going through the standard questionnaire in court, but I was a little misled by my public defender. Honestly, I was ill-informed. They gave me two and a half years under the maximum, so legally not excessive—but that doesn’t mean it was fair.

McGuire, the prosecutor for the state? Didn’t ring a bell. The real DA on my felony case was Denis John Stingl, who made an unofficial offer of “five in, five out.” When I asked my lawyer to get it in writing, he said the DA had mixed up the cases, so no deal.

The plea was presented to me as-is by my attorney and in court with an initialed questionnaire clarified on record. I wasn’t at any off-the-record meetings and didn’t hear about any, except for one mishap. All I have are transcripts, police reports, and official stuff.

I’m not innocent, though. Not in the saintly sense. I messed up. But this fight isn’t about perfection—it’s about fairness. I needed a wake-up call, and this system gave me one in the worst way possible.

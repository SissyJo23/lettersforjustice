🗝️ The Lighthouse Keeper’s Hope

Sent: 5/21/2025

A bond endures where justice once was lost,

He asked the night from his home, soft and low:
The windows open to the breeze's flow,
The scent of rain on pavement, fresh and clear,
A world rebuilt, yet haunted by the near.

"Is this how it ends—I'm free, and she...?"

From her lighthouse, gazing out with care,
She stands where salt-wind tangles in her hair.
Orange blossoms scent the cool night air,
Their sweetness drifting, mingling with despair.

Below, the trains wail softly as they leave,
A melancholy music, hearts to grieve.

She whispers to the silence, lost in thought,
Her voice a fragile echo, barely caught:
"He's home, but is the fight for him really...?"

The fight for justice won—so now, what's left?
Is it for him, or just the love they kept?

Did they know the other loved, or was it hidden—
A secret whispered, lost beneath the battle,
A spark unspoken, waiting in the middle?

Or did each heart, in messages and sighs,
Guess at the truth, yet fear to realize?

So now she wonders, as the night drifts by,
If love was ever more than hope's reply.

Their questions hang, unanswered, in the sky.

Yet in their hearts, the messages remain,
Each text and chat, alive with joy and pain.
A love unnamed, a hope that will sustain,
Each word a bridge across the miles of rain.

Though never met, their voices softly blend,
A tale of whispers carried by the frost.
A whispered story with no certain end.
